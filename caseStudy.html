Amazon Redesign

Amazon is a site that we take for granted. It changed the eCommerce world and our expectations of what a great online experience can be. I’ll admit I was intimidated when my group of designers were asked to redesign three user flows - Searching, Purchasing, and Creating An Account.

Since the brief was completely open and we had a deadline quickly approaching, my group saw the benefits of putting in place constraints that would enable to focus our energy and attention on the experience rather than wasting time on things like designing a new logo or an entirely new design language. So we put in place three constraints.

  1. Our design will look and feel like an Amazon product page
  2. Our design experience will cross devices
  3. Our design will utilize atomic design (components)

Now that we understood what success would look like, we started by doing a SWOT analysis of Amazon’s product page.

Strength Weakness Opportunity Threats
Brand Recognition Inundated with information More user control Walmart
Strong eCommerce Platform Excess features Eliminate less useful info/features Target
Ratings & Reviews Trust Reviews & Ratings Navigation Highlight price/product comparisons Ebay

From our intital look at Amazon, we saw there were was some opportunity around user control and configuration. We also realized there was something to a dual shopping experience. We didn't know what it was, but we knew we wanted to find out. That led us to our research objectives.

  • Online shopping habits and pain points
  • The online purchasing experiences
  • Online shopping with someone else in mind
  • Interviews: 15
  • Survey responses: 106
  • Survey age range: 19-68

a comfortable experience where they don’t feel like they’re being asked to complete a task

a shared purchasing experience that doesn't take them offsite

Hypothesis: It would be valuable to develop a new type of shared Amazon account for those who make buying decisions together. When two people sign up on a shared account, they would then be able to share items with each other immediately and split the total cost check-out. We believed this would be helpful for couples who shop together online, siblings who want to find and split a gift for Mother’s Day, and similar scenarios where more than one person would be part of the shopping process.
